### Title
DTQN for FoV‑Constrained Navigation: Memory‑Augmented Subgoal Selection in 2D with Extensions to 3D Point‑Cloud Robotics

### Abstract (150–200 words)
- Problem: Safe navigation under severe partial observability from limited field‑of‑view (FoV).
- Approach: A hierarchical two‑level stack: a DTQN high‑level subgoal selector operating on short observation histories, coupled with a low‑level controller that tracks waypoints while respecting dynamics and safety.
- Model I/O: Input is a sequence of k time steps of a 16‑D feature vector per candidate; output is a scalar Q at the final step used to rank subgoals.
- Deployment: 2D navigation with visibility/occlusion constraints; extension to 3D via Unity‑ROS and real point‑cloud pipelines.
- Results: Strong gains over classical planners and RL baselines in success rate, safety margins, and time‑to‑goal; ablations confirm the role of memory, hierarchy, and candidate‑set design.
- Key novelty statements.

### III. Problem Formulation
- Formalize navigation as a POMDP with FoV‑limited observations.
- State and observation: 2D features for candidate subgoals; occlusion and “enemy visibility” constraints.
- Action space: discrete subgoal selections (high‑level) and/or low‑level actions (variant).
- Reward: goal progress, collision penalty, visibility penalty (being “seen”), action efficiency.
- Metrics: success rate, time‑to‑goal, path length, min obstacle distance, visibility exposure, collision rate, compute rate (Hz).
- Equation box: Q‑learning objective with causal modeling of history.

### IV. Method
- Hierarchical stack overview:
  - High level: DTQN‑based subgoal scorer selects a waypoint from a candidate set under FoV and visibility constraints.
  - Low level: trajectory follower (PID/MPC/velocity commands) that tracks the chosen subgoal and handles short‑horizon collision avoidance.
  - Safety layer: candidate masking via line‑of‑sight, obstacle clearance, and visibility exposure limits.
- DTQN architecture and I/O:
  - Input per candidate: sequence length k of a 16‑D feature vector [agent_x, agent_y, vel_x, vel_y, goal_dx, goal_dy, dist_goal, cand_x, cand_y, agent_to_cand_dx, agent_to_cand_dy, dist_cand, num_enemies, min_enemy_to_cand_dist, num_enemies_seeing_cand, any_enemy_sees_agent].
  - Positional encoding for k steps; causal multi‑head attention layers; linear head.
  - Defaults from implementation: d_model=64, n_heads=4, n_layers=3; output shape (B, k, output_dim); we use the last step.
  - Output: scalar Q per candidate at the final step; ε‑greedy or argmax selection.
- Candidate generation in 2D:
  - Free‑space polygon decomposition and subgoal centroids along safe frontiers; invalidate candidates occluded or too close to obstacles/enemies.
  - Mask rules from implementation: avoid candidates within a small radius of current location; avoid candidates farther than the final goal distance.
  - Novelty: visibility‑aware scoring features and exposure‑aware masking integrated into the RL training signal.
- End‑to‑end system pipeline (2D → 3D):
  - Perception: 2D map or 3D point cloud; persistence filtering of transient obstacles.
  - Candidate set: generate safe candidates; compute 16‑D features for each.
  - DTQN scoring: tile features to k, infer Q; choose best candidate.
  - Control: low‑level controller executes towards chosen subgoal; force↔velocity mapping for ROS cmd_vel; odom feedback closes the loop.
  - Anticipatory safety: predicted enemy FOV unions treated as temporary obstacles to shape low‑level forces.
  - 3D extension: point‑cloud to features via voxelization/range‑image/slices projected into the same 16‑D schema for DTQN reuse.
- Training details:
  - Replay buffer with sequences of length k; target network; MSE loss on TD targets; online updates during rollout with next‑candidate max for bootstrapping.
  - Reward shaping: goal progress, collision penalty, visibility exposure penalty, control smoothness.
  - Curriculum: increase clutter/enemy density and narrow FoV over training; stochastic observation dropouts to mimic sensor gaps.
  - Optimization: Adam, learning rate schedule; gradient clipping; mixed‑precision optional; periodic checkpointing and CSV logging of decisions/Qs.
